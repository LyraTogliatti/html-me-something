The Games That Inspire Me

When I was a child, I would often wonder what secrets developers hid in the game I was playing. Programming and game development were worlds of infinite possibilities and nothing gave me more of a drive to continue playing. Bloodborne is the first game in my life to fully warrant this idea. Years after release, new bosses are still being discovered in the Chalice Dungeons; cut characters are changing the context of the story, while hidden voice files are rewritting it entirely. Bloodborne is the gothic game that never stops letting me dream.

Yoko Taro, Director at Square Enix, is rapidly becoming known for his works that ask greater questions than media has previously answered. With Nier, his beloved spinoff to the Drakengard franchise, he asked his players a question of perspective in conflict - in times of war, can either side truly be in the right? Nier: Automata elevates these ideas, telling a complex story of human morals through the androids they've created.

Few games have told a more "classic" story than Life is Strange while also breaking the mold in depiction of human interaction. Life is Strange is not afraid to tell its players stories of queer characters, mental health issues, drug abuse - and succeeds at creating accountability for not protecting those they love.
